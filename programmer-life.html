My Journey As A Programmer

By Ebenezer Ajewole on June 21, 2020 • 5 mins read
Scroll

I like my choices.

It was in September 2019 during my industrial training, i had just finished a level of training in 3D modelling which involved creating 3 dimentional images of objects using an engineering computer aided design software.

I'm an enthusiat and i love exploring new options. The engineering designs came quite easy for me due to the fact that i have an handle on other design softwares but programming? Not in my wildest dreams did i even consider it.

I created abstract 3D models of objects and 3D-printed them using my supervisor's 3D printer at his office.

Then, a friend who was a senior colleague and a full-time staff of the agency asked me if i knew any programming languages. I didn't like the question because i hate saying no to questions that question my intellectual ability.
So guess what?
I neither said no nor yes. I immediately asked him if he could teach me.

Mr Kelvin smiled (knowing he had a serious job he had been working on for a long time) and said to me; "guy, you can learn it anywhere". "Go online and look for materials to build yourself with".
My next question was; what do i start with?

You see, i believe in the saying of Alvin Toffler. He said; "The illiterate of the 21st century will not be those who cannot read and write, but those who cannot learn, unlearn, and relearn"

I like my choices. It always involve unlearning old traditions and belief systems that were never true, and learning new things that are related to personal development and the society.

I started by learning the HyperText Markup Language (HTML) and adding simple styles with the Cascading Style Sheet (CSS). Over just a year, i moved into writing more confiscated codes which involves backend development and databases.
All i needed wasn't even just a push but an awareness that there is something i still didn't know... That's what drives me, i want to know more...
